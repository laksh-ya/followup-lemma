# Acme Financial Services — Standard Payment Terms

Version 2.0 · Applies to all standard service agreements unless a contract overrides.

## 1. Net terms

- Default invoicing term is **Net-30**: payment is due within 30 calendar days of the
  invoice date.
- Select enterprise accounts operate on **Net-45** where specified in their MSA.
- The due date printed on the invoice is authoritative.

## 2. Late payment

- Invoices unpaid after the due date are "overdue" from day 1.
- A late-payment service charge of **1.5% per month** (18% per annum) may be applied to
  balances overdue beyond 15 days, at Acme's discretion. Mention this only from Stage 3
  onward, and only as a possibility ("may be applied"), never as a fixed penalty.
- Continued non-payment beyond 21 days may result in a **hold on credit terms**, meaning
  future work moves to advance payment.

## 3. Accepted payment methods

- Online payment link (preferred) — included on every invoice and follow-up.
- Bank transfer (NEFT/RTGS/IMPS) — details available from the finance team on request.
- Cheque — by prior arrangement only.

## 4. Payment confirmation

Clients are asked to share a payment reference or UTR number once payment is made so the
account can be reconciled and follow-ups stopped promptly.

## 5. Currency

Invoices are issued in INR unless the contract specifies otherwise. Amounts in
follow-ups must match the invoice currency and value exactly.

## 6. Questions and disputes

Billing questions should be raised within 7 days of the invoice date. A raised dispute
pauses late charges on the disputed portion until resolved.
