# Acme Financial Services — Accounts Receivable Collection Policy

Version 3.1 · Owner: Finance Operations

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs how Acme follows up on overdue customer invoices. The goal is to
recover outstanding amounts while preserving the customer relationship and staying
within professional and legal boundaries. Every follow-up must be accurate, respectful,
and proportional to how overdue the invoice is.

## 2. Escalation stages and tone

Follow-ups escalate with the number of days an invoice is past its due date. The tone
must match the stage exactly — never threaten at an early stage, never sound casual at a
late one.

| Stage | Days overdue | Tone | Intent |
|-------|--------------|------|--------|
| Stage 1 | 1–7 | Warm, friendly | Assume an oversight; a gentle nudge |
| Stage 2 | 8–14 | Polite but firm | Request a confirmed payment date |
| Stage 3 | 15–21 | Formal, serious | Note impact on credit terms; 48-hour response window |
| Stage 4 | 22–30 | Stern, urgent | Final automated notice; 24-hour window before manual escalation |
| Escalated | 30+ | No automated email | Hand to the legal/recovery team |

## 3. Hard rules for every follow-up

1. Never invent or alter invoice data. Use only the invoice number, amount, due date,
   and days overdue exactly as provided.
2. Never mention or threaten legal action before Stage 4.
3. Always include the payment link if one is on file; otherwise direct the customer to
   contact the finance team for bank details.
4. Never be abusive, sarcastic, or unprofessional, regardless of how overdue the invoice.
5. Always address the client by name and reference the specific invoice number.
6. Keep emails concise: a subject line and a short, scannable body.

## 4. Stage-specific guidance

- **Stage 1 (Warm):** Assume the client simply forgot. No pressure. "Just a friendly
  reminder." Thank them in advance.
- **Stage 2 (Polite but firm):** Acknowledge the earlier reminder. Ask for a specific
  expected payment date. Stay courteous.
- **Stage 3 (Formal & serious):** State this is a repeated notice. Note that continued
  non-payment may affect their credit terms with Acme. Request a response within 48 hours
  with either payment confirmation or a resolution plan.
- **Stage 4 (Stern & urgent):** This is the final automated reminder. Give a clear
  24-hour deadline. State that the next step is manual escalation to the finance/legal
  team. Remain professional — firm, not hostile.

## 5. VIP and high-value accounts

For clients marked VIP, soften the tone by one notch and always offer a direct line to
the finance lead. For invoices above ₹200,000, a human reviews any Stage 3 or later
notice before it is sent.

## 6. Disputes and promises to pay

If a client disputes an invoice, set the account to DISPUTED and stop automated
follow-ups until a human resolves it. If a client promises a payment date, record the
promise and pause escalation until that date; if the date passes unpaid, resume at the
current stage.

## 7. Legal escalation

Invoices 30+ days overdue are removed from automated email and flagged to the legal
team. No automated message is sent at this stage. The legal team decides next steps.
